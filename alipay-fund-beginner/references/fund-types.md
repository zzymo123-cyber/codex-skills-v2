# Fund Types

Use these plain-language classifications when reviewing Alipay fund pages or holdings.

## Common Types

- 货币基金: Usually used for cash management. It tends to fluctuate less than stock funds, but it is still a fund product, not a bank deposit.
- 纯债基金: Mainly invests in bonds. It can still lose money, especially when interest rates, credit risk, or liquidity conditions change.
- 一级债基/二级债基: Bond funds that may have different degrees of equity-like exposure. Do not treat them as the same as pure bond funds.
- 固收+: A "bond plus" style product. The plus part may include stocks, convertible bonds, or other risk assets, so it can fall more than a pure bond fund.
- 指数基金: Tracks an index. Risk depends on the index. Broad indexes are usually more diversified than industry indexes.
- 主动权益基金: A manager actively selects stocks. Volatility and manager/style risk can be significant.
- 行业/主题基金: Focuses on one industry or theme, such as healthcare, technology, new energy, consumer, military, or semiconductors. Concentration risk is high.
- QDII/海外基金: Invests outside mainland China. Consider overseas market risk, exchange-rate risk, time-zone differences, purchase limits, and higher tracking delays.
- 商品/黄金/原油基金: Driven by commodity prices and sometimes futures structures. It may be very volatile and should not be treated as ordinary "stable" allocation.
- FOF: Invests in other funds. Look through to the underlying fund types when possible.

## Conservative Classification Rules

- If the name contains a hot industry or theme, treat it as potentially concentrated until proven otherwise.
- If a product says "固收+" or "稳健", still check whether it holds stocks, convertible bonds, or high-yield credit.
- If the user cannot identify the fund type, label the next step as `先学习再决定` or `需要补充信息`.
- If two funds track similar indexes or hold similar top positions, treat them as possibly overlapping even if their names differ.
